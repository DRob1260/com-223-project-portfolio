Rosetta@home: Fighting COVID-19 with Computers

In order to join the fight against COVID-19, Team 777 contributed to the Rosetta@home project. Rosetta@home allows anyone to download an app on their computer or Android phone that automatically runs "protein-folding" simulations. These simulations provide valuable data about proteins involved in dangerous diseases, including the proteins involved with the recent pandemic. The eventual end result of Rosetta@home's protein folding is enabling scientists to find weaknesses in the virus for drugs and/or vaccines to target.

The Goal
  • Specific: Recruit people to join the Rosetta@home cause, volunteering their computers to run protein folding simulations that are used for COVID-19 medical research.
  • Measurable: The Rosetta@home website will keep track of who has joined our team and how many ‘credits’ has been awarded.
  • Attainable: It is within our abilities to recruit people and assist them in setting up their computers to run the Rosetta@home program.
  • Realistic: Most modern computers are capable of running the program for Rosetta@home and we know many friends, family, and peers who have computers. It is reasonable to expect that we can convince some of them to support the cause for COVID-19 medical research.
  • Time-bound: From 3/30 - 5/1, we will each each spend at least 1 hour every week recruiting people to join our Rosetta@home team and assist them in getting set up.
The Plan
  • We will individually recruit people to join the Rosetta@home cause and assist them in getting set up for at least 1 hour each week.
  • Recruiting efforts may include:
    • Texting/calling friends, family, and peers who may be able to and interested in donating their compute time.
    • Posting on social media.
    • Any other creative/effective methods.
  • We will individually keep track of time spent and who we have recruited (noting each person’s username).
  • We will join the team to donate our compute time if/when possible.

Explanation

In just a month's time, Team 777 was able to form a team of 13 protein folders to contribute a hefty chunk of simulation data towards the COVID-19 research community. The charts and statistics above are meant to guage a level of impact made by the team while pursuing its mission. The numbers look great and show real progress over time, but how much has the team helped with COVID-19 research? The protein folding tasks performed by Team 777 and its recruitees provided a unique service for biomedical scientists. The entire Rosetta@home community forms a virtual supercomputer capable of running the millions of calculations needed to determine protein interactions. Only time will tell but Team 777's efforts have real chance at helping scientists find the cure to the world's current pandemic.
